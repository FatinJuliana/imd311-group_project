15 Fun Facts About Cat


As far as we know that the cat is a spoiled pet just like a dog. However, cats actually have facts related to them that not everyone knows. Cats can not only be a place to play together, but also able to relieve stress on humans. Cats are pets that can provide peace of mind while relieving the stress that humans go through.


1. Cats can detect earthquakes. Logically, it can also make sense. But in theory, its validity has yet to be confirmed. According to Reader’s Digest sources, cat footprints are able to detect impending earthquakes. The soles of the cat's feet are a sensitive part of the body and sensitive to earthquakes.


2. Cats can swallow and digest food without having to chew.


3. Cats also dream. Naturally we know that cats like to fall asleep even for a moment. But when the cat sleeps soundly, it also produces sensory waves in their brain just like when humans are asleep.


4. Cats do not understand why they are punished if they make a mistake. They should be praised and rewarded for the desired behavior or otherwise.


5. Cats sniff each other's noses when they meet. It was one of the ways they got to know each other better. However, not all cats will do this. Only cats that already know each other dare to do so. It helps them to identify their acquaintances as well as know the personality of the cat more deeply.


6. Scientists are still unable to identify the cause of cats 'purring'.


7. Cats will produce 'purring' when they sigh and also exhale carbon dioxide.


8. Young cats have a monotonous ‘purring’ tone compared to older cats.


9. They still maintain the kitten’s vocal rate to communicate with its owner.


10. Just like dogs, cats can also get sick or die if they eat chocolate.


1. A cat will close or blink its eyes if inadvertently he has eye contact with a human. If you are trying to tame a feral cat, try blinking your eyes and looking the other way while staring into his eyes.


12. Give cats raw meat every day to keep their gums and teeth healthy and strong.


13. Cats can receive heat or heat well because originally the descendants of cats in ancient times were animals that lived in the desert.


14. The cat's pulse beats about 160-240 beats per minute. It depends on the age of the cat. The older a cat is, the slower its pulse rate.


15. Cats also sweat like humans. But not on his fur or skin. Instead they sweated through his soles. If you feel moisture around the cat’s feet, give them a little soaking in water so the cute one can stay hydrated.
