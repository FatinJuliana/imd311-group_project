Popular Cat Breeds in Malaysia


If you are interested in keeping or owning any of these cat breeds, you have come to the right place! Here, you will find everything you need to know about this charming family of cats. As we cannot discuss all types of cats in Malaysia, this article contains the 8 most common cat breeds found in Malaysia.


1. Maine Coon


It has long fringed and thick fur, which requires frequent management, with its large head, high ears, and wide chest further adding to its elegant features. It is an energetic agile cat that will need exercise to stay healthy and happy, but it is also a friendly, sociable, and always calm cat. It is also easy to train due to the ingenuity it has! Unfortunately, due to its size, it is easy to suffer from hip dysplasia and can get obesity problems. In addition, it can also get heart disease and urinary tract infections.


2. Persian


This breed has wide and short legs, strong soles, and long fur where it is easy to ‘matting’, as well as with its most distinctive features of having small distant ears and a short nose. There are Peke-faced Persians cats that are flat-faced, and also Classic Persians that are less flat-faced with more prominent features. Other variations include Teacup Persian, Doll-face Persian, Chinchilla Persian and Exotic Shorthair. Due to its short nose, it is easy to have trouble breathing and even get eye pain. It is also relatively easy to get affected by heat and can have sensitive skin.


3. Ragdoll


Ragdoll’s name comes from its floppy and relaxed, good nature. Laid-back and intelligent, Ragdolls have captivating blue eyes and distinct colourpoint coats. Their silky, plush semi long coats require minimal grooming to keep the coats in good condition. Ragdolls are well-behaved and easy to care – that’s why they are well-loved by people.
Origin: California, United States of America
Life Span: 15 to 20 years
Weight: Up to 20 pounds


4. Bengal


This cat has short shiny fur, which comes in different colors namely brown, silver and white. It has a long body but a small dome-shaped head. It also has large soles, distinctive knuckles, and a thick round -shaped tail. It is a friendly cat, who loves everything! Even water too! It is also an energetic and intelligent active breed, therefore mind stimulation activities are obligatory. Please note that it has a sensitive stomach. Plus, it’s easy to get cancer, pancreatic disease and feline urinary tract disease.


5. Exotic Shorthair


Exotic Shorthairs is sometimes called as “the lazy man’s Persian”. Unlike Persians, Exotic Shorthairs have short and plush coats that are easier to take care. It’s a great choice for those who love the Persian’s docile personality but find it hard to take care of its long fur coat. The coats of Exotic Shorthairs come in a variety of colours such as white, cream, black and beige. Since they are lap cats with affectionate personalities, they don’t like to be alone. Calm and loyal, they are friendly to other pets including canines. However, they may take a little time to get comfortable with someone new.
Origin: United States of America
Life Span: 10 to 15 years
Weight: Up to 14 pounds


6. Burmese


It is a skinny and agile cat, who is often friendly, hospitable and spoiled. The color of the fur is dark on the part near the face, ears, and tail, with light blue or golden brown eyes. This cat is known for its extremely loyal nature to its master; you will find it can’t get away with you in too long. Like Bengal cats, this breed has a sensitive stomach, accompanied by a tendency to develop eye problems that will lead to blindness, asthma and hip dysplasia.


7. American Shorthair


This breed is one of the Malaysian cat breeds that originated from Europe and America. Historically, its strong jaws and powerful body have been used as pest control. This breed is calm and plays well on its own. Plus, it’s a simple-friendly cat that will love every member of your family. Unfortunately, it can lead to health problems through hereditary factors such as heart problems, hip dysplasia and obesity. Online, these cats can be priced from RM300 to RM2000.


8. Norwegian Forest


Commonly called as Norwegian Forest Cat, the Skogkatt is a large, semi long-haired cat with charming personalities that match its stunning look. Sweet and gentle, it originated in Norway. The Skogkatts are featured in mythology and folk tales for centuries Well-known for being tolerant toward children and other pets, they are one of the best family companions.
Origin: Norway
Life Span: 14 to 16 years
Weight: 7 to 20 pounds
